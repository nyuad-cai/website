About Us

In the era of big data and artificial intelligence, computational precision health can create opportunities that directly contribute to saving human lives, improving efficiencies of care providers, and decreasing the high burden of disease and socioeconomic costs. Led by PI Farah Shamout, the long-term goal of the Clinical AI Lab is to improve health outcomes by designing and developing smart diagnostic and prognostic systems. Our research interests cut across multiple disciplines, namely health, engineering, and computing.

Our research agenda consists of two interrelated tracks:

  • conceptual machine learning frameworks to address current limitations of existing approaches

  • key thematic application areas mainly related to clinical decision support systems, cancer, and women's health.
