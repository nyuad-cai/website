PhD Opportunities

We are currently hiring PhD candidates via the NYU Global PhD Fellowship for either the (i) NYU Tandon Biomedical Engineering program or (ii) the NYU Tandon Computer Science & Engineering program.

Biomedical Engineering, PhD



Students holding a BS degree in biomedical engineering or a related field of science or engineering are eligible to apply to the BME Ph.D. Program. Acceptance to the program will depend on academic excellence, research interests and recommendations. Read more about this opportunity here.





Computer Science, Ph.D.




The NYU Computer Science Ph.D. program seeks students holding Undergraduate and Master's degrees. The Program gives students the chance to do their research abroad under the supervision of faculty at NYU Shanghai or NYU Abu Dhabi. It also offers scholarships that cover tuition and monthly stipend. Find more about the program here.



Visiting Graduate Students Opportunities

We occasionally hire graduate (MSc and PhD) students as visiting scholars. If you are interested, please share your CV, cover letter, and transcripts via email. More information on how to contact us here.

Full-time opportunities

The clinical AI Lab is hiring for the positions of Post-Doc Associate, Research Assistant, and Research Engineer.

Post-Doctoral Assistant in the Division of Engineering



The Clinical AI Lab seeks to improve patient care by developing new machine learning (ML) methodologies that tackle unique computational problems in healthcare applications. We are hiring a Postdoctoral Associate to join the team and make significant contributions to the field. The researcher is expected to have (i) strong machine learning skills to improve model accuracy, robustness, and explainability, and (ii) exemplary passion to pursue research at the intersection of computing and healthcare. Read more here.





Research Engineer in the Division of Engineering



We are seeking a Research Engineer to join the Clinical AI Lab. The researcher is expected to have (i) strong engineering skills to find innovative and cost-effective means to improve our research, and (ii) exemplary passion and motivation to pursue multidisciplinary research at the intersection of computing and healthcare. Methodologies of interest include: multi-modal learning, transfer learning, graph neural networks. Find more information here.





Research Assistant in the Division of Engineering



The Clinical AI Lab are seeking a Research Assistant to join the team. The researcher is expected to have (i) hands-on experience with machine learning and data science via course projects, internships, or work experience, and (ii) exemplary passion and motivation to pursue multidisciplinary research at the intersection of computing and healthcare. Students who currently have or are in the process of completing a Bachelor’s or a Master’s degree in Computer Science, Mathematics, Computer Engineering, or relevant technical fields are eligible to apply. Find more information here.
